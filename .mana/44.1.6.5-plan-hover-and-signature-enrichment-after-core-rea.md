## Goal
Plan the enrichment slice for Phase 1 read-oriented code intelligence in `imp`: hover and signature.

## Current state
The read-oriented plan includes hover and signature in Phase 1 but places them after diagnostics, symbols, and xref because they are enrichment surfaces rather than the core proving slice.

## Scope
- Planning only.
- Cover hover cards, signature help, transcript truncation rules, and cross-linking from diagnostics or call sites.
- Keep the slice read-only and backend-agnostic at the runtime contract level.

## Acceptance
- The slice identifies the normalized model-facing payloads for hover and signature surfaces.
- It records how these should enrich, rather than replace, definition/reference/diagnostic flows.
- It defines compact transcript summaries and when verbose documentation should move to a viewer or secondary inspection path.

## Important constraints
- Do not let verbose documentation payloads dominate the default shell transcript.
- Do not treat hover/signature as justification to skip deterministic verification or diagnostics.
- Keep the phase order explicit: these follow the core navigation/query surfaces.
