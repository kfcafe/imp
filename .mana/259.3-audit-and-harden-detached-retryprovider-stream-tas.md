After hook supervision cleanup, audit and harden detached/background spawned tasks in provider streaming and retry pipelines.

Goal:
- Inventory spawned tasks in `imp-core::retry` and `imp-llm` streaming providers.
- Distinguish acceptable channel-owned background workers from important tasks whose panics can currently vanish.
- Convert important detached task failures into supervised/reportable failures where practical, without broad provider rewrites.

Current state:
- `imp-core/src/retry.rs` spawns a forwarding worker around retry stream construction.
- `imp-llm/src/providers/{anthropic,openai,openai_compat,google}.rs` use spawned stream/event forwarding tasks.
- These tasks may be channel-owned and acceptable in some cases, but they need an explicit policy and any important silent-failure paths should be tightened.

Work:
1. Inspect spawned task sites in retry + providers.
2. Classify each site as:
   - acceptable best-effort/channel-owned worker
   - needs supervision/join handling
   - should convert panic/failure into streamed error/event
3. Implement the smallest high-value fixes for any clearly unsafe detached paths.
4. Record decisions/remaining acceptable exceptions in unit notes so future workers know the policy.
