## Goal
Decide later whether repeated promoted code-intelligence summaries justify a small shared durable contract between `imp` and `mana`.

## Current state
`docs/design/code-intelligence-evidence-promotion.md` deliberately avoids introducing a schema now. The accepted planning position is that code-intelligence is transient by default in `imp`, and only promoted consequences belong in `mana` as notes, verify evidence, facts, or decisions.

## Task
When enough real promotion cases exist, evaluate whether the repeated durable summary shape is stable enough to type.

## Required analysis
1. Inspect actual promoted examples from future implementation work, especially verify/check summaries and diagnostic blocker summaries.
2. Compare the repeated fields across examples.
3. Recommend either:
   - no shared schema yet, or
   - a minimal durable contract limited to promoted summaries.
4. If recommending a contract, keep it explicitly consequence-oriented and exclude live runtime/LSP/session state.

## Candidate later fields
- source_kind
- source_name
- scope
- outcome
- summary
- affected_paths
- optional affected_symbols
- artifact_refs
- provenance

## Do not
- Do not model live language-server state.
- Do not model per-turn diagnostic streams.
- Do not move code-intelligence orchestration into `mana`.
- Do not introduce a broad schema just because typing feels cleaner.

## Relevant files
- `docs/design/code-intelligence-evidence-promotion.md`
- `docs/design/code-intelligence-strategy.md`
- future promoted evidence/fact records under root `.mana/`

## Acceptance
The resulting recommendation should be grounded in real promoted examples and make a clear yes/no call on whether a tiny shared durable contract is warranted.
