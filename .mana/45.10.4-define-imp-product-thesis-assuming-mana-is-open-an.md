  - Open product questions to resolve next: what `more opinionated than pi` should concretely decide for the user; what `more streamlined than hermes` specifically means; and whether the next architecture focus should shift from broad mana-platform refactor talk to the imp product loop itself.

  2026-04-16T08:53:22.610320+00:00
  User converged on wanting an explicit imp workflow loop. Preferred first-pass 4-part loop is: `/ask` → `/plan` → `/work` → `/improve`.
  - `/ask`: clarification / scoping / requirements gathering.
  - `/plan`: shape the implementation approach; visible plan mode is okay but automatic planning is also acceptable when work is small or obvious.
  - `/work`: execute the task.
  - `/improve`: review, refine, harden, and iterate after the first working result.
  User specifically identifies the workflow shape itself—not delegation/team/runtime details—as the most compelling aspect of OMX.
  This suggests the next product-definition step should be to define the semantics, transitions, and auto-escalation rules for this explicit 4-part imp loop, and then map mana integration onto it rather than the other way around.

   - autonomous worker/runtime quality
