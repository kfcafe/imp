## Goal
Plan the project-scale browsing slice for Phase 1 read-oriented code intelligence in `imp`: find references and workspace symbols.

## Current state
The read-oriented plan puts these after common normalization and after symbol/definition support because they produce larger result sets and need stronger grouping and handoff rules.

## Scope
- Planning only.
- Cover reference-hit normalization, workspace symbol results, grouping, truncation, pagination/handoff, and viewer-oriented inspection.
- Keep the slice read-only.

## Acceptance
- The slice explains how references and workspace symbols should be summarized without transcript overload.
- It records grouping by file/symbol/source and the threshold for `imp view` or equivalent inspector handoff.
- It identifies usage-kind fields or graceful `unknown` fallback when semantic backends do not classify references fully.

## Important constraints
- Do not collapse large result sets into noisy raw transcript dumps.
- Do not assume all languages can support full semantic usage classification on day one.
- Keep this downstream of the symbol identity/definition groundwork.
