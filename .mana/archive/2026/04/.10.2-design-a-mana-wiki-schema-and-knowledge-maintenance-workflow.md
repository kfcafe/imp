## Goal
Define the synthesized knowledge layer that sits on top of mana existing units and facts. Current mana usage in imp gives us a durable work graph and some verified facts, but not a maintained project wiki that compounds understanding over time.

This is inspired by Karpathy LLM Wiki idea: instead of rediscovering project knowledge from scratch each session, an LLM incrementally builds and maintains a persistent interlinked wiki. The key adaptation for mana: the wiki should leverage mana verification model where possible, and clearly distinguish verified facts from synthesized summaries from open hypotheses.

## Scope
- In scope: propose a `.mana/wiki/` layout, page types, metadata/frontmatter conventions, citation/source rules, and maintenance operations.
- In scope: define how the wiki relates to raw mana units, facts, closed child jobs, and logs.
- In scope: define trust tiers such as verified facts, synthesized pages, and open questions/hypotheses.
- Out of scope: implementing mana-core commands or building a search engine.

## Deliverable
- A proposal doc at `docs/proposals/mana-wiki-schema-and-workflow.md`.
- The doc should be written as an implementation-ready spec for future runtime/prompt work.

## Patch Guidance
- Docs-only patch.
- Keep mana itself the durable substrate; do not accidentally redesign mana into a separate database product.
- Be explicit about what remains in unit files versus what becomes wiki content.

## Focus
- Relationship to `.10.1` memory architecture decisions.
- Existing mana concepts already used in imp: units, facts, archived work, child-job synthesis.
- How a future imp agent would perform `ingest`, `query`, and `lint` style operations safely.

## Required Questions to Answer
1. What page types should exist first (systems, concepts, decisions, playbooks, incidents, etc.)?
2. How are strong claims cited back to units/facts/files?
3. What should be stored as a fact instead of freeform wiki prose?
4. What operations should maintain the wiki (`ingest`, `query`, `lint`) and when should they run?
5. Should this be a mana-core feature, an imp-maintained companion layer, or a phased hybrid? State the recommendation clearly.
